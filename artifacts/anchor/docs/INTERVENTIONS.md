# Anchor — Intervention Reference

## 1. Urge Surfing

**Duration:** 5–10 minutes  
**Origin:** Mindfulness-Based Relapse Prevention (Marlatt & Gordon)

The craving is a wave. It rises, peaks, and passes — usually in under 20 minutes. The goal is to observe it without being swept away.

**Steps:**
1. Name the urge: "I notice I'm having an urge to use."
2. Observe where it lives in the body (tightness, heat, restlessness)
3. Breathe and watch it as if from shore
4. Note that it rises and falls
5. Remind: "I don't have to act on this. It will pass."

## 2. 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding

**Duration:** 2–4 minutes  
**Origin:** Sensory grounding for anxiety/trauma management

Brings attention from urge/anxiety into the physical present.

**Steps:**
1. Name 5 things you can SEE
2. Name 4 things you can TOUCH (and touch them)
3. Name 3 things you can HEAR
4. Name 2 things you can SMELL
5. Name 1 thing you can TASTE

## 3. Box Breathing

**Duration:** 4 minutes (or more)  
**Origin:** US Navy SEALs, clinical PTSD/anxiety treatment

Activates parasympathetic response, interrupts fight-or-flight.

**Pattern:**
- Inhale: 4 counts
- Hold: 4 counts
- Exhale: 4 counts
- Hold: 4 counts

Repeat 4–8 cycles. App guides visually with a slow expanding/contracting square.

## 4. Cold Water Reset

**Duration:** 30 seconds–2 minutes  
**Origin:** TIPP skill (DBT), mammalian dive reflex activation

Physical interrupt that rapidly reduces emotional intensity.

**Options:**
- Splash cold water on face
- Hold wrists under cold running water
- Hold ice cube in each hand
- Submerge face in cold water bowl

Note: Not suitable for people with heart conditions. Framed as "one option to try."

## 5. Play the Tape Forward

**Duration:** 3–8 minutes  
**Origin:** Cognitive rehearsal, Motivational Interviewing

Walks the user through the likely downstream consequences of using, then contrasts with choosing not to use.

**Steps:**
1. Imagine taking the first step toward using
2. Follow the mental movie forward — hour 1, hour 2, tomorrow, next week
3. Notice feelings at each point
4. Now rewind: choose not to use
5. Follow that tape forward instead
6. Notice how that feels

## 6. Self-Compassion Reframe

**Duration:** 3–5 minutes  
**Origin:** Kristin Neff's Self-Compassion Framework

Counteracts shame spiral, which is a major relapse driver.

**Steps:**
1. Acknowledge: "This is really hard right now."
2. Normalize: "Many people struggle with this — I'm not alone."
3. Kindness: "What would I say to a close friend in this moment?"
4. Speak that to yourself.

## 7. Distraction / Redirection

**Duration:** Variable  
**Purpose:** Pattern interrupt, short-term craving displacement

Simple activity suggestions to redirect attention for 15–20 minutes:
- Go for a walk (even around the block)
- Text or call someone (not to talk about urges, just to connect)
- Make a cup of tea
- Do 20 jumping jacks
- Watch something funny
- Doodle or draw something
- Clean one small area

## Usage in App

Each intervention is accessible from the main "I Need Help Now" screen. Users can tap any card to start it. Progress is not tracked in ways that create pressure. Completion simply returns to the home screen with a brief affirmation.
